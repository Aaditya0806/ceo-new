What is SBPS?

The Skill-Based Progression System (SBPS) is a systematic framework designed to identify, develop, and recognize the technical and functional skills necessary for various job roles. Structured around the LEEP Model—Learn, Execute, Excel, and Propagate—SBPS aims to empower employees while supporting operational excellence and organizational flexibility.

The Four Levels of Proficiency

Learn

Understanding the basics of a skill or process.

Execute

Applying acquired knowledge in daily tasks

Excel

Demonstrating expertise and adherence to quality standards .

Propagate

Sharing expertise by training and mentoring others.

Objectives of SBPS

Enhance Multi-Skilling

Develop versatile employees capable of handling multiple functions. Build a flexible workforce that adapts to dynamic organizational needs.

Foster Career Progression

Provide clear career growth paths. Equip team members with skills for higher responsibilities and job rotations.

Improve Performance

Close competency gaps through targeted training. Align individual skills with organizational objectives for continuous improvement .

Benefits of SBPS

For Employees

Provides clarity on career progression and skill expectations. Encourages continuous learning and skill enhancement. Increases job satisfaction through empowerment and recognition.

For Organizations

Builds a pipeline of versatile, high-performing employees. Reduces dependency on external hiring, fostering internal growth. Enhances operational efficiency and readiness to face challenges.

Mentorship Initiatives

Mentorship is a key element in SBPS, providing employees with real-time guidance and support. Experienced mentors help mentees bridge skill gaps, improve performance, and align individual goals with organizational objectives. This collaborative approach ensures a win-win scenario for both employees and the organization.

FAQs

The Skill-Based Progression System (SBPS) is a framework for developing, assessing, and recognizing the skills of employees, fostering career growth and organizational excellence.

The LEEP model defines four levels of proficiency: Learn (L1), Execute (L2), Excel (L3), and Propagate (L4), allowing employees to progress and share their expertise.

SBPS offers career clarity, continuous learning, and increased job satisfaction through empowerment and recognition.

It builds a flexible and highly skilled workforce, reduces reliance on external hiring, and enhances operational efficiency.
